About

Hi! I'm Cyrus, a senior at The Harker School. I'm broadly interested in building a "grand unified theory of human disease biology" — combining physical and computational frameworks to understand the macro and microscale organization of the body. My research has been recognized in the Regeneron Science Talent Search, International Research Olympiad, and the International Conference on Neurology and Brain Disorders.

I'm also deeply passionate about healthcare accessibility and fixing systems-level inefficiencies that prevent therapeutics from reaching the people who need them. I've spoken at the UN High-Level Political Forum '25 and organized the WHO-Africa x Federation of African Medical Students' Associations General Assembly on the topic for thousands of participants internationally.

Outside of research and advocacy, I love reading thought-provoking blogs and novels. If you have any recommendations, I'd be happy to trade — so please feel free to send them my way!

Research

  • Toward a Unified Model of the Macaque Cortex with Multi-Scale Spatiotemporal-Neurochemical Integration
    Harvard OpenBio Student Research Institute
    Developed a unified macroscale model of the brain across space, time, and neurochemistry for the first time using macaque fMRI data. Won 1st place in original research for my paper and poster presentation. Later recognized as a Regeneron Science Talent Search Scholar and presented at The Global Health Leaders Conference at Johns Hopkins University and the 10th Edition of the International Conference on Neurology and Brain Disorders.
    paper · github
    2024
  • Molecular and Genetic Pathways for rpsE-Mediated Spectinomycin Resistance in Vibrio natriegens
    Summer Science Program
    Probed the molecular and genetic basis for antibiotic resistance in Vibrio natriegens. Built an analysis pipeline with NumPy, Galaxy, and BLAST for DNA sequencing analysis and quality checks, and AlphaFold3 for protein structure prediction and visualization.
    paper (embargoed) · poster
    2025
